How much money did Colorado actually get?

A COVID-19 funding tracker from the Peter G. Peterson Foundation estimates that Colorado received $63.17 billion in funding from 22 major federal programs, which ranked 20th among U.S. states. The funds can be put into five buckets:

Personal

Colorado residents received some $23 billion in direct federal aid, the bulk in economic impact payments from the IRS in 2020 and 2021. The unemployed received about $9.1 billion in enhanced benefits. Another $690 million was provided in rental assistance. Those funds are still being distributed.

Business

Colorado businesses received more than $21 billion in federal assistance from the U.S. Small Business Administration. The vast majority, $15.1 billion, came through the Paycheck Protection Program, which provided forgivable loans if the funds were spent on payroll costs.

Government

State and local governments in Colorado received nearly $11 billion in federal aid, including FEMA funds and Coronavirus Relief Funds to deal with public health impacts of the pandemic. Last year, they received another $5.8 billion to backfill lost revenues and to fund recovery efforts.

Health care

Colorado health care providers received $5.1 billion in relief funds and advanced Medicare payments. The HHS Provider Relief Fund, the most widely accessed program, helped more than 6,000 dentists, doctors, clinics, and other healthcare providers recover lost revenues.

Education

Primary and secondary education providers, primarily school districts, in Colorado received $1.8 billion, while universities and colleges received $955 million. The money was used on protective measures and to support the shift to remote learning, as well as other programs.

Which counties got larger shares of the money?

Federal pandemic distributions across Colorado were uneven, and they often varied based on how actively businesses sought help from SBA lending programs. On a per-capita basis, mountain resorts received much more aid than rural areas that were less dependent on tourism.
